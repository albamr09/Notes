Unbiased and Objective Data

  • Bias: preference in favor or against a person or thing
  • Data Bias: type of error that systematically skews results in a certain direction
  • Unbiased sampling: when a sample is representative of the population being measured

To avoid bias when sampling, make sure to take random samples and make visualizations comparing the population with your sample to check if the sample is representative.

Types of bias:

  • Sampling Bias: when a sample is not representative of the population as a whole
  • Observer Bias (experimenter bias/research bias): tendency for different people to observe things differently
  • Interpretation Bias: tendency to always interpret ambiguous situations in a positive or negative way
  • Confirmation Bias: tendency to search for or interpret information in a way that confirms pre-existing beliefs
